But, after all this, what is all about the glitch and Databending? Well, someone wrote on Wikipedia "Databending (or data bending) is the process of manipulating a media file of a certain format, using software designed to edit files of another format. Distortions in the medium typically occur as a result, and the process either falls under a broader category of, or is frequently employed in glitch art." I think this is a good explanation and it's quite concise but i will add a few personal toughts...

Databending is a very interesting manifestation of digital art. It consists of treating image files (.JPEG, for example), as if they were audio. To those of audio, as if they were video, and so on as far as the imagination covers.The result is full of new palettes, of random, industrial color associations, like the shuffle of our players. So experimentation is all about when we try things that work (at least partially) on a random basis.

Data bending is usually related to the aesthetics of glitch, but it is a type of error caused, not accidental wich is the main difference. In any case, the results are always unexpected, since transcoding is done automatically or semi-automatically and it is practically impossible to foresee its consequences.
